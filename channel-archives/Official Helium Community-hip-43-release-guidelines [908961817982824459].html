Here is what I had written up I had written this a while ago, but this channel just popped up so I guess it is time to share and hopefully get some feedback. TLDR: 1. rollout should be limited to a monthly basis -makes for less downtime 2. Each vendor should push OTA updates in groups of 4, over a minimum of 4 days. -2 reasons: (1). Areas with high density population of vendor's hot spot will have less of an effect on other hotspots on the area, as far as downtime goes. (2). It will allow for critical errors to not put the whole network down. All feedback is welcome :) https://docs.google.com/document/d/1Gm7_NvZ2QZOcRcWhWcOb8RSwxv9DH_q2u_iwANdmu7w/edit?usp=drivesdk

JGRAYBKK- Official HNT Runner Up 11/13/2021 1:37 PM
Glad you asked @Fizzy Basically I live in a very densely Bobcat populated area. Many times, when there was a Bobcat OTA, all my witness went offline during that and pretty much crippled my earnings. This was for sure multiple times a month and sometimes multiple times per week. I have been thinking about this for some time now but the real catalyst was when that update came out ~2 weeks ago that was taking hotspots offline. It made it obvious that these rollouts have to have a staggered rollout. By doing so it would allow for more stability in the rollout of updates by only having a portion of hotspots updating at one time. AND if there was a critical issue then it could (hopefully) be stopped before it affects a majority of one vendor's hotspots or even the network as a whole.

ⓈⒽⒶⓇⓀ 12/27/2021 10:41 PM
It seems like the goal for manufacturers should be MTTR (Mean Time to Recovery) after an update. It doesn't matter how often manufacturers update their devices as long as the quickly are back online and participating in the network. I wouldn't care if there were 10 updates per day, as long as data transfers and mining wasn't impacted. Right now, the lack of any MTTR focus is why so many miners are offline all the time (especially RAKs which can take days to recover) after any outage/update/etc. Bobcat and SenseCAP have introduced auto-fast sync features which help drastically reduce MTTR.
